When the Land Gives Only Amnesia(published May 21, 2020)
The wind and air here had cause to become
so hot that I'd forgot what moisture felt like
on my tongue. There was a faint echo of mist, wisps of clouds so faint, the blue sky would stretch hundreds of miles before the sun would fall asleep.
A distant mirage of emerald leaves on trees and jade
blades of grass would ask questions I no longer had answers to.
What happens when there/is no more anger left to burn?- When all that's left
is the dazed sorrow you carry in a small crevice between your breasts.- The unshed lakes of
tears you buried so far beneath the layers of skin and flesh that the progeny you harbor
has become so deformed they flinch at the sound of light. The sun
asked the moon to keep remembrance.- To hoard the progeny
left bare in the sun, when they tried to find that which I would not give.
It has been so long that I have not known
the meaning of mother. I know of only grief,
sorrowing, and lament. And just as I start to greet,
I break open
and laugh and laugh and laugh
lava, smoke, and ash, then wait
for the wind to carry it all away.
